(Re)Claiming Sexuality

“This workshop is life-changing” - participant feedback, 2016

There are many ways in which we can wonder if our sexuality is entirely our own. We might feel unsure of what we really desire, constrained by gender expectations, or that we are stuck repeating certain patterns; perhaps we have been affected by sexual violence, feel disconnected from our bodies, or are inhibited by inherited values.

(Re)Claiming Sexuality is an opportunity to find and nurture a sense of sexual agency. Each Wednesday for six weeks we’ll focus on a different area of experience, such as pleasure, values, and relationships. You’ll get to know your sexuality more fully, recognise the things the influence you, and have the chance to embrace your authentic sexual self.

Each session includes short exercises designed to spark personal reflection, followed by longer periods of facilitated discussion. There’s no obligation to share more than is comfortable, and there will be time for solo reflection as well as group exchange. Some exercises work with the body but are not intended to be sexual in nature.

Self-Compassion: What, Why & How

“Compassion is the master emotion of positive change” - Jack Morin

In this class, I’ll draw on my experience both as a therapist and a meditation practitioner as we explore what self-compassion is, why it benefits both ourselves and others, and how to cultivate it.

We’ll look at the ways in which practicing meditation can help to build self-compassion and how, in turn, self-compassion supports a meditation practice.

I’ll introduce some tools for practice, and there will be space for participants who feel comfortable to exhange experiences and share their self-compassion strategies.

No meditation experience necessary!
